Resource • Puppy & Kitten Tips

Your first-year roadmap.

Vaccines, nutrition, socialization, and easy habits that set your new best friend up for success.

See the timeline Ask a question

Big wins in the first month

✅ Start a routine

Same meal times, potty breaks, and sleep schedule = faster learning and less stress.

✅ Make the vet “normal”

Short happy visits + treats make future appointments calmer.

✅ Train micro-habits

Touch paws, ears, mouth gently every day for 10 seconds — it pays off forever.

First-year timeline (simple version)

8–12 weeks

Foundation

  • Core vaccines begin (series)
  • Parasite prevention plan (flea/tick/heartworm)
  • Nutrition guidance + safe chews
  • Socialization (gentle + positive)
12–16 weeks

Confidence & habits

  • Vaccine boosters as scheduled
  • Basic training: name, recall, leash skills
  • Handling practice: brushing, nails, teeth touch
4–6 months

Growing up

  • Teething support + safe chew options
  • Discuss spay/neuter timing (individualized)
  • Keep building calm grooming routines
6–12 months

Maintenance

  • Adult diet transition timing (breed/size matters)
  • Yearly wellness rhythm starts forming
  • Dental habits: brushing, dental chews, rechecks

Tips that save you later

Brush teeth early (even if imperfect)

Start by touching lips → gums → quick brush. Consistency beats perfection.

Dental resource →

Make meds easy before you need them

Practice “open mouth + treat” so giving meds later isn’t scary.

Giving meds resource →

Socialize smart

Exposure should be positive and controlled — not overwhelming. Small wins build brave pets.

Reminder: Vaccine schedules depend on age, risk, and lifestyle. Your vet will tailor it.

Resource • Giving Medications

Make meds less stressful.

Simple techniques for pills, liquids, and “nope” pets — without turning it into a wrestling match.

Quick start Need help?

Quick start (works for most pets)

1) Stay calm

Keep your voice soft. The goal is “boring and normal.”

2) Reward first

Give a treat BEFORE you medicate, so good things are already happening.

3) Be fast

Prepare everything ahead of time so the “moment” is 5 seconds.

Pro move: use “chaser treats” — med → tasty treat → another tasty treat.

Pills & capsules

Best for: tablets, capsules, chewy meds.

Method A: Hide it

  • Use a pill pocket, cheese, peanut butter (xylitol-free), or wet food.
  • Start with a “fake” treat (no pill) so they trust it.
  • Follow with 1–2 extra treats so they swallow.

Method B: Place it

  1. Hold the upper jaw gently and tilt nose slightly up.
  2. Open the lower jaw and place pill far back on the tongue.
  3. Close mouth, stroke throat once, then offer a treat.

Don’t pry hard. If your pet panics, switch to “hide it” or ask us about alternatives.

Liquid medications

Best for: syringes, suspensions, liquid antibiotics.

Less mess technique

  1. Insert syringe into the side of the mouth (behind canine tooth).
  2. Aim toward the cheek pouch — not straight back.
  3. Give small bursts, letting them swallow between.
  4. Finish with a treat or a little food.
If your pet coughs, gags repeatedly, or liquid comes out the nose — stop and contact your vet.

For “spicy” cats (and tiny dogs)

The goal is control + comfort, not force.

Towel wrap (“purrito”)

  • Lay towel flat, place pet centered with head out.
  • Wrap snugly around shoulders/arms to limit paw swats.
  • Medicate quickly, then release + reward.

Ask about options

  • Compounded flavors
  • Transdermal gels (when appropriate)
  • Different forms (chew/liquid)

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Chasing your pet around first (they learn to hide)
  • Giving meds when you’re stressed or rushed
  • Skipping the reward “because they don’t deserve it”
  • Crushing meds without checking (some should NOT be crushed)
  • Mixing in a huge bowl of food (they might not finish it)
Important: Always confirm with your vet before crushing, splitting, or mixing medications.

Still struggling?

You’re not doing anything “wrong” — some pets just need a different plan. Reach out and we’ll help.

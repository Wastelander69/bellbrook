Puppy & Kitten Tips

Your first-year roadmap: wellness visits, vaccines, nutrition, and confidence-building habits.

Start strong (and keep it simple)

New pets learn fast — and so do humans. This guide is built around small, repeatable steps: gentle routines, early prevention, and visits that feel calm. (Demo content — swap with real clinic guidance any time.)

First Week Must-Dos
  • Schedule your first wellness visit
  • Choose a food and keep it consistent
  • Start crate/carrier “happy time”
  • Pick a parasite prevention plan
Habit Builders
  • Touch paws/ears gently every day
  • Short sessions: brush, inspect teeth
  • Reward calm behavior
  • Practice leash/carrier routines
Red Flags to Call About
  • Not eating for a full day
  • Repeated vomiting/diarrhea
  • Extreme lethargy
  • Trouble breathing or collapse

Vaccines & prevention

Puppies and kittens need a series of vaccines as their immune system develops. Your vet will tailor timing based on age, lifestyle, and local risk. Parasite prevention is often year-round — ask what’s right for your pet.

Nutrition made easy

Pick a complete, age-appropriate diet and stick with it for a couple weeks before making changes. If you switch foods, do it slowly over 7–10 days. Treats are great — keep them small and consistent.

Comfort at the vet

The goal is a “this place is safe” vibe. Bring a favorite treat, use a secure carrier/leash, and ask about happy visits. Calm reps = confident pets.

Dental Health

Fresh breath isn’t just “nice” — it can be a sign of comfort, health, and fewer infections.

Back to Resources Watch Dental Videos

What dental disease looks like

Dental issues are super common and often painless at first — which makes them easy to miss. Early care can prevent discomfort, infections, and tooth loss. (Demo info — always follow your vet’s advice.)

Bad breath that doesn’t improve
Red or bleeding gums
Drooling or pawing at mouth
Trouble chewing / dropping food
Visible tartar buildup
Swelling near the jaw
Quick tip
If breath suddenly gets worse, don’t wait — it’s often the earliest sign something’s brewing.

What a professional cleaning is like

  1. Exam + planning: your vet checks teeth/gums and discusses options.
  2. Cleaning: tartar is removed above and below the gumline.
  3. Dental x-rays (when indicated): helps find hidden problems.
  4. Polish: smooths the tooth surface to slow plaque buildup.
  5. Home plan: easy steps to keep results longer.

At-home dental routine (simple version)

  • Brush 3–5x/week if possible (even 2x/week helps)
  • Use pet-safe toothpaste only
  • Start with a fingertip rub if brushing is too much
  • Dental chews can help (ask what’s safe for your pet)
  • Re-check at wellness visits
Pro move
Keep sessions short. Touch → treat. Brush → treat. Stop while it’s still “easy.”

Why dental care matters

Dental disease is extremely common in pets. Plaque and tartar can lead to gum inflammation, pain, tooth loss, and bacteria that may affect overall health.

Rule of thumb: if breath is “really bad,” gums bleed easily, or chewing seems painful — it’s worth a dental check.

Signs to watch for

  • Bad breath that doesn’t go away
  • Red gums or bleeding
  • Drooling, pawing at the mouth
  • Chewing on one side / dropping food
  • Yellow-brown tartar buildup
  • Swelling near the face or under the eye

At-home tips that actually help

  1. Start slow: let your pet lick pet-safe toothpaste off your finger.
  2. Build up: touch the gums and outer teeth for 5–10 seconds.
  3. Brush outer surfaces: focus where plaque builds fastest.
  4. Keep it short: 30–60 seconds is a win.
  5. Reward: treat + praise every time.

Dental chews and water additives can help, but brushing is the gold standard.

What happens at a dental cleaning?

Cleanings are done under anesthesia so we can scale above and below the gumline, take dental x-rays, and treat painful problem teeth safely.

Before

Exam + planning, pre-anesthetic labwork when appropriate.

After

Polish, home-care plan, and follow-up guidance.
